Research Works & Publications Ongoing Research

Optimization of Selective Laser Sintering (SLS) 3D Printing of Thermoplastic Polyurethane Elastomer: A Statistical Approach

Published in Journal of Manufacturing and Materials Processing

This research seeks to optimize selective laser sintering process parameters for TPU powder to achieve high-quality 3D printed parts. This involves determining ideal settings for layer thickness and laser power through modeling, experimentation, and mechanical fatigue and fracture analysis to ensure durability under real-world conditions.

Comparative Analysis of KNN and SVM in Multicriteria Inventory Classification Using TOPSIS

Published in International Journal of Information Technology

This research compares ML models to identify the optimal model for rapidly classifying new inventory in a multicriteria ABC system. By leveraging an initial TOPSIS analysis and evaluating SVM and KNN accuracy in predicting classes for new items, the goal is to find the most accurate model which will enable efficient categorization of newly added items based on multiple properties, avoiding disruption in competitive environments.

Advancing Breast Cancer Prediction: Comparative Analysis of ML models and DL-Based Multi-Model Ensembles on Original and Synthetic Datasets

Published in PLOS One

This research aims to enhance breast cancer diagnosis through a comparative evaluation of various machine learning and deep learning techniques using both original and synthetic datasets. The goal is to identify an optimal approach or combination of approaches that can improve prediction accuracy and contribute to the advancement of breast cancer diagnosis.

Supplier selection using machine learning integrated fuzzy AHP and Bayesian Belief Network approach: Case study of a jute company

Under Review in Expert Systems With Applications

This research seeks to optimize jute yarn supplier selection for a Bangladeshi company by integrating fuzzy AHP, Bayesian belief networks, and machine learning techniques. The goal is to develop an accurate data-driven model for weighing criteria, assessing supplier performance, analyzing risks, and identifying ideal suppliers to improve procurement decisions.

Machine learning-based models for cardiovascular disease prediction

Under Review in Informatics in Medicine Unlocked

This research seeks to identify the optimal machine learning model for accurately diagnosing cardiovascular disease early and conduct risk analysis using Bayesian belief networks on the optimal model. By determining the highest-performing ML approach, the aim is to develop an effective disease prediction system that improves clinical decision-making and patient outcomes through early detection and intervention.
